Indian Tea: Heritage, Quality & Global Reach

Tea cultivation in India began in the 1820s when the British East India Company established plantations in Assam, building on native traditions. From these origins, India has grown into one of the world’s largest producers and exporters of tea.

In 2024, India exported around 255 million kg of tea, valued at over US $850 million, with exports continuing to rise by nearly 3% in FY 2024–25.

India’s teas are prized worldwide — the bold strength of Assam, the delicate Darjeeling, the fragrant Nilgiri, and the organic Temi (Sikkim), many of which hold Geographical Indication status. Alongside these, India produces Green and Oolong teas that are gaining strong traction in premium markets.

Export varieties include Black Tea (≈96%), along with Green, Oolong, Masala, Herbal, and Lemon teas, serving diverse global tastes.

Black Tea

Black Tea is the country’s most widely produced variety, making up nearly 96% of exports. Known for its depth and diversity, it ranges from strong and malty to delicate and aromatic, reflecting the unique terroirs of India’s tea regions.

Assam Black Tea is bold, full-bodied, and malty, forming the base of breakfast blends worldwide. Darjeeling Black Tea, called the “Champagne of Teas,” is delicate, floral, and prized for its muscatel flavor. Nilgiri Black Teas offer a fragrant, brisk cup with bright liquor, while Dooars and Terai varieties are smooth, balanced, and strong.

Hand-plucked and traditionally processed, Indian Black Tea is offered in orthodox, CTC, and specialty grades—delivering consistent quality for both premium single-origin teas and blended products.

Arabica Coffee

Indian Arabica, mainly grown in Karnataka, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu, accounts for about 30% of India’s coffee production and is renowned for its smooth, aromatic flavor. Shade-grown in high-altitude estates, it offers a mild, well-balanced cup with hints of fruit, chocolate, and spice. Ideal for specialty brews and single-origin offerings, it delivers elegance and complexity. With India’s coffee exports surpassing US$1.2 billion in FY 2023–24, Arabica continues to attract global buyers seeking premium quality and distinctive taste.

Instant Coffee

Indian Instant Coffee is crafted from high-quality Robusta and Arabica beans, selected for consistency and flavor. Carefully roasted and freeze- or spray-dried, it delivers a rich, aromatic cup that dissolves instantly without compromising taste. Known for its full-bodied, smooth profile with chocolatey and nutty notes, it is ideal for busy lifestyles and global markets seeking convenience without sacrificing quality. India’s instant coffee exports are growing steadily, reflecting rising worldwide demand for premium soluble coffee.

Indian Specialty Coffee

Indian Specialty Coffee comes from high-altitude estates in Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu, and unique regions like Araku Valley and Coorg. Carefully hand-picked, shade-grown, and processed with precision, these beans offer complex flavors, bright acidity, and aromatic depth, making them ideal for single-origin and specialty brews. Recognized for quality and traceability, Indian Specialty Coffee continues to gain global acclaim, reflecting India’s growing reputation in the premium coffee market.
