India’s Coffee: Heritage, Quality & Global Reach

Coffee in India traces back to the 1600s, when Baba Budan introduced beans from Yemen to Chikkamagaluru, later flourished into commercial estates during the 1800s.

Today, India exports both Arabica (≈30%) and Robusta (≈70%), producing globally admired varieties like Coorg, Chikmagalur, Bababudangiris, Araku Valley, Wayanad, and the unique Monsooned Malabar.

Exports crossed US$1.2–1.3 billion in FY 2023–24, with Europe as the leading market, and long-term growth continues despite seasonal fluctuations.

Indian coffee is mostly shade-grown, hand-picked, and carefully processed, giving it a distinctive full-bodied flavor with spice-toned notes.

Combining heritage, quality, and diverse origins, Indian coffee stands as one of the world’s most reliable and unique export offerings.
